Beyond Blur: A Fluid Perspective on Generative Diffusion Models

ICCV 2025

Grzegorz Gruszczynski1,2, Jakub Jerzy Meixner1,3, Michal Jan Wlodarczyk1,4, Przemyslaw Musialski1,5,6,
1IDEAS NCBR, 2Samsung AI Center Warsaw, 3Polish Academy of Sciences, 4Warsaw University of Technology, 5IDEAS Research Institute, 6New Jersey Institute of Technology
Paper arXiv

BeyondBlur generalizes existing approaches and proposes a new physics inspired way to think about corruption.

Abstract

We propose a novel PDE-driven corruption process for generative image synthesis based on advection-diffusion processes which generalizes existing PDE-based approaches.

Our forward pass formulates image corruption via a physically motivated PDE that couples directional advection with isotropic diffusion and Gaussian noise, controlled by dimensionless numbers (Péclet, Fourier). We implement this PDE numerically through a GPU-accelerated custom Lattice Boltzmann solver for fast evaluation. To induce realistic “turbulence,” we generate stochastic velocity fields that introduce coherent motion and capture multi-scale mixing. In the generative process, a neural network learns to reverse the advection-diffusion operator thus constituting a novel generative model.

We discuss how previous methods emerge as specific cases of our operator, demonstrating that our framework generalizes prior PDE-based corruption techniques. We illustrate how advection improves the diversity and quality of the generated images while keeping the overall color palette unaffected. This work bridges fluid dynamics, dimensionless PDE theory, and deep generative modeling, offering a fresh perspective on physically informed image

Contribution

We design a PDE-based forward corruption process for diffusion models that combines directional advection, isotropic diffusion, and a small Gaussian perturbation. The image field \(u(x,y,t)\) evolves according to

\( \dfrac{\partial u}{\partial t} + \nabla\!\cdot\!(v\,u) \;=\; \nabla\!\cdot(\alpha\,\nabla u) \;+\; \dot{Q}(t) \)
  • Advection (\(\nabla\!\cdot(vu)\)) - shifts intensities along a turbulent velocity field \(v\), producing coherent, structured motions while preserving the global “mass” (color sum).
  • Diffusion (\(\nabla\!\cdot(\alpha\nabla u)\)) - smooths local variations, also conservative.
  • Reaction (\(\dot Q(t)\)) - a small Gaussian noise term added only before the reverse network.

In our forward trajectory, advection is introduced in controlled amounts, serving primarily to induce variability in the corruption paths and to study the influence of the advection term. This produces directional, flow-like texture changes beyond what isotropic blur can achieve. Gaussian noise remains minimal, ensuring that the forward process stays physically consistent and that the global color palette is preserved.

We implement this PDE numerically using a GPU-accelerated Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM), which efficiently handles the coupled advection–diffusion dynamics at image scale. Advection is driven by a spectrally generated turbulent velocity field with controllable multi-scale energy, enabling resolution-independent control of flow patterns through two dimensionless parameters:

  • Fourier number (Fo) — total diffusion per step.
  • Péclet number (Pe) — ratio of advection to diffusion.

When \( \mathrm{Pe}=0 \), the method reduces to prior isotropic PDE-based corruptions (e.g., heat-equation blur), making our approach a superset of earlier models.
